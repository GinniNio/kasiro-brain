# Kasiro Differentiated Market Strategy
**Domain owner:** Market Brain | **Version:** 1.0 | **Last updated:** 2026-07-09

---

## Strategic Objective

Kasiro must not become a smaller African copy of a sportsbook or a global prediction exchange. The board should be recognised for questions that convert African attention, money, technology, public life and culture into clean, tradeable outcomes.

The differentiator is the combination of:

1. Nigeria-first and Africa-relevant subjects
2. Public, auditable resolution sources
3. Distinctive market structures
4. Strong editorial framing
5. Small, mixed-category publishing waves
6. Better question design and settlement discipline than local competitors

Ordinary winner markets remain useful as anchors. They must not define the board.

---

## Five Editorial Lenses

Every market should fit at least one recurring editorial lens.

### 1. Attention Africa
Measures what Africans search for, watch, discuss, follow or adopt.

Examples:
- Which story leads Nigerian Google interest during a defined week?
- Which newly revealed reality-TV participant gains the most followers in 72 hours?
- Which African artist gains the most public attention during a fixed release window?

Preferred sources: Google Trends Topics, Wikimedia Pageviews, public chart archives, timestamped official-account snapshots.

### 2. Money and Daily Life
Tracks inflation, FX, fuel, listed equities, consumer costs and policy consequences.

Examples:
- What happens first after a CBN decision?
- Which NGX sector performs best in a defined month?
- Which official Nigerian economic threshold is crossed first?

Preferred sources: CBN, NBS, NGX, official commodity or regulator datasets.

### 3. Sport as a Story System
Builds event families and tournament narratives instead of publishing only match winners.

Examples:
- Group-stage points ranges
- Tournament baskets
- First-event races
- Qualification paths
- Team performance indexes

Use direct match markets as entry products. Add only props supported by a dependable official data provider.

### 4. Politics in Motion
Focuses on formal declarations, coalition formation, legislation, ratification, appointments and election sequences.

Examples:
- Which political event happens first?
- How many state assemblies ratify an amendment?
- Which party announces a complete ticket first?

Avoid personality gossip, vague removal rumours and distant candidate pools with fabricated options.

### 5. Future Africa
Tracks AI, startups, digital identity, telecoms, satellites, infrastructure, science and public technology.

Examples:
- Which Nigerian digital milestone is reported first?
- Which African country leads disclosed startup funding?
- Which major African technology milestone happens first?
- Which AI model performs best on a published Nigeria benchmark?

Preferred sources: NCC, NIMC, company filings, regulators, exchanges, Africa: The Big Deal, Partech, Briter Bridges, peer-reviewed or official scientific sources.

## Signature Market Formats

Market Brain should actively search for these formats before defaulting to a binary question.

### Race
Question: Which qualifying event happens first?

Requirements:
- Outcomes must be mutually exclusive at the moment of first occurrence.
- Include a deadline and a `None by [date]` outcome where necessary.
- All competing events need comparable source quality.

Best uses: politics, technology launches, infrastructure milestones, corporate filings, records and attention milestones.

### Basket
Question: How many of a predefined set of events occur?

Requirements:
- Freeze the event list before trading opens.
- Use non-overlapping numeric ranges.
- Define treatment of cancellations, ties and missing data.

Best uses: tournament performance, earnings, chart penetration, scientific milestones and policy adoption.

### Attention Battle
Question: Which topic, person, artist or event receives the most measurable attention in a fixed window?

Requirements:
- Use a single metric and source.
- Predefine exact Google Topics, page names, chart names, accounts or hashtags.
- Fix start and end timestamps.
- Define ties before publication.

Best uses: culture, entertainment, politics, sport and technology.

### Reaction Market
Question: What happens immediately after a known decision, release or announcement?

Requirements:
- Freeze the baseline value before the event.
- Define the first complete trading day or measurement window.
- Prevent trading after the underlying decision begins leaking.

Best uses: CBN decisions, earnings, regulatory actions, product launches and elections.

### Index Market
Question: What combined result emerges from several observable components?

Requirements:
- Publish the formula and component weights before opening.
- Use objective inputs only.
- Avoid operator discretion after trading starts.

Best uses: sentiment, tournament performance, business health, technology adoption and public-service reliability.

### Threshold Ladder
Question: Which price or metric levels are reached by a deadline?

Requirements:
- Distinguish independent binary thresholds from mutually exclusive closing bands.
- Name the exact price series, observation time and timezone.
- Do not mix official, retail and parallel-market figures.

Best uses: FX, NGX, commodities, inflation and subscriber statistics.

### Deadline Ladder
Question: Will an event occur by each of several nested dates?

Requirements:
- Earlier YES implies later YES.
- Each contract must use the same qualifying-event definition.
- A licence, intention or pilot does not equal commercial launch unless stated.

Best uses: IPO filings, satellite launches, appointments, legislation and product availability.

### Event Family
One major event supports a small linked set of markets.

Recommended composition:
- One accessible anchor market
- One path or performance market
- One distinctive basket, race or reaction market
- Maximum one data-heavy prop unless the oracle is dependable

Avoid publishing many correlated props that fragment thin liquidity.

## Market Selection Score

Score every candidate from 0 to 5 on each dimension:

| Dimension | Test |
|---|---|
| Audience pull | Will a broad Nigerian or African audience understand why this matters now? |
| Distinctiveness | Does the question feel recognisably Kasiro rather than copied from a sportsbook? |
| Verifiability | Is there a primary or high-quality public source that resolves the exact claim? |
| Trading tension | Is there credible uncertainty, ideally with a defendable prior between 20% and 80%? |
| Shelf life | Can it remain relevant long enough to attract liquidity without becoming stale? |
| Operational load | Can a solo operator monitor, close and resolve it reliably? |
| Shareability | Can the market question itself function as compelling social content? |
| Portfolio fit | Does it improve category balance and avoid duplicating the live board? |

### Decision bands

- **34–40:** flagship candidate
- **28–33:** publishable after validation and pricing
- **22–27:** watchlist or redesign
- **Below 22:** reject

A candidate automatically fails regardless of score if the source, close-time safety or resolution criteria fail.

## Validation Doctrine

Every proposed fact must be rechecked online in the current session. Prior notes, screenshots, competitor cards and earlier drafts are discovery inputs only.

For every candidate, verify:

1. The event exists and has not started or resolved.
2. The public schedule or deadline is current.
3. Named participants, companies, candidates, tracks or products are real and eligible.
4. The proposed source publishes the exact settlement variable.
5. The close time precedes information leakage.
6. The outcome set is exhaustive or includes `Other`/`None` where justified.
7. The question is not already live in materially similar form.
8. The market is not controlled by a participant who can cheaply manipulate the result.
9. Historical data can be recovered after the event, not merely viewed live.
10. Pricing is separated from factual validation.

Never present an opening price as verified unless a current benchmark or defendable model has been run. Idea generation and pricing are separate passes.

## Source Hierarchy

Use sources in this order:

1. Official regulator, organiser, exchange, company filing or government publication
2. Official chart, platform analytics page or public API
3. Reuters, AP or another high-quality wire for event confirmation
4. Established specialist dataset with published methodology
5. Major local publication as supporting context

A news article may confirm a storyline. It should not replace the official resolution source when one exists.

### Source-specific rules

- **Google Trends:** Use predefined Topics where available; freeze geography, search type, category and measurement window.
- **Spotify:** Public chart rankings are acceptable. Exact historical stream totals require an approved analytics source or timestamped capture system.
- **Social followers/posts:** Require exact official accounts and timestamped operator snapshots. Treat as secondary inventory due to deletion and manipulation risk.
- **Startup funding:** Use one tracker throughout the market and freeze its treatment of debt, grants, exits and undisclosed rounds.
- **Fuel and retail prices:** Name one official or repeatable price series. Do not mix refinery gantry price with retail pump price.
- **Politics:** Formal declaration means an official party, candidate, court, legislature or electoral-body publication, not anonymous reporting.

## Portfolio Construction

### Publishing waves

Publish 3–5 markets per wave across at least three lenses. Do not release a large undifferentiated batch.

A preferred wave contains:

- One mass-audience anchor
- One culture or attention market
- One money/business market
- One politics or public-affairs market
- One Future Africa market

### Board balance

No single subject family should dominate more than roughly half of the visible board outside a major tournament window.

Sports can acquire users. Culture, money, politics and Future Africa create broader identity and repeat visits.

### Recurring franchises

Develop repeatable named products:

- **Nigeria Attention Index:** What will Nigeria search for or discuss next?
- **After the Decision:** Market reactions following CBN, earnings or regulation.
- **Future Africa Race:** Technology, infrastructure and startup milestones.
- **Africa Crossover:** African culture performance on global charts and platforms.
- **Tournament Pulse:** Baskets and paths across major African competitions.

Each franchise must use stable measurement rules so users learn the format.

## Competitor Doctrine

Competitor boards are used to discover:

- formats that generate repeat trading;
- topics with proven local demand;
- missing category coverage;
- pricing references where contracts are equivalent.

Do not copy:

- zero-price or broken candidate pools;
- `ends by outcome` with no real close time;
- sensational rumours;
- overlapping outcomes;
- unverifiable social metrics;
- creator-controlled activity merely because it trades elsewhere;
- large numbers of distant political futures that immobilise liquidity;
- sports props whose official data cannot be sourced.

Kasiro’s advantage is better market design, sourcing and editorial identity.

## Explicit Holds and Rejections from the Session

Hold until source or event validation is complete:

- Victor Osimhen destination pools with named clubs
- Named Nollywood annual box-office winner pools
- Nollywood versus Hollywood market-share market
- Dangote petrol below a threshold
- Nigeria’s next unicorn candidate pool
- Flutterwave IPO filing market

Reject under current operating conditions:

- Private or unrecoverable Spotify stream totals
- Markets based on fabricated or unverified film titles, candidates or deal values
- Social activity markets with no timestamp capture process
- Subjective culture or AI claims with no published benchmark
- Thresholds that combine incompatible price series
