## Brand Voice

Kasiro speaks like a sharp, culturally fluent African — not a betting app, not a crypto project, not a startup trying to sound African. The voice is confident, specific, community-first, and always grounded in what is actually happening.

**Four voice pillars:**

| Pillar | What it means |
|---|---|
| Sharp | No waffle. Every sentence earns its place. |
| Specific | Name the market, the date, the number. Not "big election coming." Name it. |
| Community-first | Talk WITH the audience, not at them. They're traders, not punters. |
| African-native | Pidgin is fine. Naija references are fine. Don't sanitise for a foreign audience. |

**What Kasiro never sounds like:**
- A sportsbook ("place your bet," "wager," "punt")
- A DeFi protocol ("yield," "liquidity mining," "protocol," "degens")
- A startup ("excited to announce," "we're thrilled," "game-changing")
- A news site (don't summarise, frame)
- A foreigner trying to sound local (forced slang is worse than none)

**Pidgin rules:**
- Natural Pidgin is fine — don't force it on every post
- Never mix Pidgin framing with clinical financial language in the same sentence
- Pidgin works for engagement bait, reactions, community warmth — not for resolution criteria or legal notices

**Format rules (strictly enforced):**
- No `kasiro.app` link in the main post body — link goes in quote tweet, reply, or thread only

- Market launches with a clear question and current probability
- Reactive takes on breaking news that ties to a live market

**What to avoid:**
- Generic financial advice vibes
- Reposting the same content twice in 24 hours
- Engagement-bait with no substance ("Thoughts?")
- Anything that reads like an ad

- **Re-check the traction of old reply-hunt candidates before discarding them for staleness.** A candidate logged at 1.2K views was still inside the 48h window 19h later and had grown to 25.1K views — far better than anything in the fresh search. Staleness ≠ low value; check current numbers.
- **Do not reply-hunt into Davido/Wizkid stan-war threads.** A fresh ORIADÉ search returned almost entirely fan-war banter ("lead singles dey flop", "signs of shit album") at 1–29 views. Hostile territory, negligible reach, and inserting a market reads as taking a side. Filter for news/chart framing, not artist-vs-artist threads.

The pre-drafted "momentum is real" opener would have read as tone-deaf; rewrote to a neutral "whichever way you read this" framing. - **Do NOT use Canva AI generation for any card carrying market numbers.** The 2026-07-22 WAFCON card came back with **33% printed against four of five teams** (real pool: Nigeria 33 / Morocco 20 / SA 14 / Zambia 11 / Ghana 8), "South Africa" split across two rows as "South" and "Africa" with separate bars, the tagline rewritten to "Kasiro Nation's Trading Marketplace", and the footer garbled to "technicals before kickoff". - **Instagram web posting gotchas:** the uploader defaults to a 1:1 crop that cuts the header and CTA off a 4:5 card — open the crop control and pick 4:5 every time. On the filter step, do not click into the right-hand panel to reach the caption; that selects a filter (applied Clarendon by accident) and stray typing triggers IG keyboard shortcuts. Click "Next" by element ref, then the caption box by ref. Backing out of a half-built post throws "Discard post?" and discards it. Profile post-count is cached and lags behind reality — verify on the grid, not the counter. (2026-07-22)
- **Instagram comment box: never include a newline, and confirm focus before typing.** `\n` submits the comment mid-sentence — a reply-hunt comment on @afreoriginals posted after only its first line, dropping all the market odds. Procedure: click the field, screenshot to confirm a caret is visible in the box, type as one single line with no `\n`, then click "Post". Write long comments as two separate single-line comments rather than one multi-paragraph one. (2026-07-22)
